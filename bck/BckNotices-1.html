          Sub: Proposed Advocate Welfare Fund Amendment Act, 2008 – present financial position of Welfare Fund and its retrospective liabilities –      concrete proposals and suggestions called for to make the fund stable at first and for further make the scheme more beneficial.

 

          On 10-6-2008 a Draft Bill was introduced to amend the Advocate Welfare Fund Act enhancing the yearly benefits from Rs. 9,375/- to Rs. 14,285/- up to a maximum of Rs. 5,00,000/-.  By the proposed amendment an additional yearly income of Rs. 1 Crore is expected.  Some Lawyer Organizations have opposed the proposed Bill and demanded for further enhancement of benefits.  Hence the Bill was not placed in the last Assembly Session.  A joint meeting of the lawyer organizations were held on 10-12-2008 at Ernakulam and the Advocate General (Chairman of the Advocate Welfare Fund Trustee Committee) presided over the meeting.

          Without making the fund stable and viable at first it is not possible for a further enhancement or taking any retrospective liability to the fund.  In view of the present financial position the proposed amendment was made only prospectively.

          The Kerala Advocates’ Welfare Fund came into force on 5-4-1981 with a total liability of Rs. 2,12,16,000/- (2.12 crores) for payment of retrospective  benefits to the then members who have previous years of practice prior to the Act 1981.  The yearly benefit in 1981 was Rs. 1,000/- per year and maximum of Rs. 30,000/- the previous years of practice prior to the Act 1981 was taken for calculation at the ratio 4:1.

          In 1989 when the Act was amended the total benefit was enhanced from Rs. 30,000/- to Rs. 60,000/- with retrospective effect.  In 1993 the maximum benefits was again enhanced to Rs. 1 lakh with retrospective effect and the ratio for calculating previous  practicing years prior to the Act was amended as 2:1 instead of 4:1.  Thereby the total retrospective liability prior to the Act 1981 came to an amount of Rs. 13,26,00,000/- (Rs. 13.26 Crores).

          Thereafter in 2000 the yearly benefit was again enhanced from Rs. 3125/- to Rs. 9,375/- and the maximum of Rs. 3 lakhs and that too with retrospective effect.  Hence the retrospective liability prior to the Act 1981 alone would come to the tune of Rs. 40 Crores.  In all the previous amendments retrospective effect prior to the Act was given and so the additional benefits for previous years prior to the Act 1981 made the fund not viable.

          Hence in the recent amendment the increased benefits would be made applicable only prospectively starting from 10-6-2008.  I hereby enclose the detailed statements regarding the annual income and annual disbursement from 2003 to 2008.

          When the new amendment was proposed by way of a Draft Bill there has been several demands from various Advocate Organizations for further increase and new benefits with retrospective effect.  The details of important amendments proposed are enclosed along with this letter.

          Before the proposed amendment there are several advocates who have exceeded 32 years of practice eligible to receive a maximum benefit of

Rs. 3,00,000/-, but still continuing payment of subscription and affixing the stamps without any benefit.  Now after the proposed amendment they would get Rs. 3,00,000/- till the date of new amendment and thereafter an amount of Rs. 14,285/- per year up to a maximum of Rs. 5,00,000/- by paying an amount of Rs. 3,000/- as yearly subscription instead of the existing Rs. 1500/- per year.  Accordingly, a fixed annual benefit of Rs. 9,375/- would be made payable for the period of practice prior to 10-6-2008 as per schedule 1 and the benefits for the period thereafter would be paid at the rate of Rs. 14,285/- upto a maximum of Rs. 5,00,000/-.

          As on 31-10-2008 there are 937 persons eligible for Rs. 3,00,000/-.  The amount required for disbursing the above said 937 persons at the rate of Rs. 3,00,000/- alone would come to a total requirement of Rs. 28,11,00,000/- (Rs. 28.11 Crores) whereas the total amount in the welfare fund as on 31-10-2008 is only Rs. 4,27,00,000/- (Rs. 4.27 Crores).  There may be other claimants having total practice less than 32 years and for disbursing such claimants further additional amount has to be found out.

          “The Bar Council of Kerala at its meeting held on 21-12-2008 resolved to forward a copy of the data relating to the brief history and financial scenario of the Welfare Fund Scheme to all the Bar Associations and Lawyer Organizations to call for their written concrete proposals.  Accordingly, I am herewith forwarding the above data along with a copy of the minutes of the joint meeting of various advocates organizations convened on 10-12-2008 at Ernakulam for your perusal and study.  I request you to go through the entire facts and circumstances of the fund and suggest your further proposal to improve the ways and means situations of the fund and thereafter to suggest further amendments to make the fund more stable and beneficial”.

          Your further proposals may kindly be forwarded within 2 weeks to the undersigned without delay.
